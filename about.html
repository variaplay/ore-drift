About Ore Drift

Last updated: April 2026

Ore Drift is a free, browser-based space mining game. Glide your ship through a field of asteroids, auto-mine ore as you pass, avoid rivals who are chasing the same rocks, and don't run out of fuel. Runs on desktop and mobile — one finger or the mouse is all you need.

What you can do

  • Drag to steer, hold to boost: the entire control scheme. Works identically on mouse and touch.
  • Auto-mine: your laser locks onto the nearest meteor on its own. No aiming.
  • Hunt crystal meteors: rare cyan rocks that pay out ~8× the ore of a normal meteor. Visible from across the field.
  • Ram rivals: slither-style — nose-first contact with another ship's body is fatal to whoever charged in. Time your boosts.
  • Grow your ship: the more ore you bank, the bigger your ship gets. A bigger ship is more intimidating — and a bigger target.

Who runs it

Ore Drift is made and operated by Variaplay LLP, an independent games studio. We build small, polished browser games and publish them under the Variaplay.com brand.

The game is free. There are no accounts, no ads, no analytics, no in-app purchases, and nothing about your play is sent to us. See our Privacy Policy for the short details.

How it's built

Ore Drift runs on Phaser 3, a 2D HTML5 game engine, loaded from a CDN. All art and sound is generated procedurally at runtime — there are no image or audio asset files. The music is a short ambient piece in A minor, synthesized live by the Web Audio API.

Ships are 32×32 retro pixel sprites with per-player color palettes. Meteor silhouettes are smooth sum-of-sine polygons, and their surface cracks grow procedurally as your laser drills through them. NPC rivals share the exact same Ship code as the player — they just steer via an AI controller instead of your finger, which keeps the codebase ready for networked multiplayer down the road.
